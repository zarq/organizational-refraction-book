## 1. Positioning statement

**Headline:** A causal theory of the strategy-reality gap — not another call to execute harder.

For years you've been told strategies fail because people don't execute. You've suspected that's not quite it — that neither the strategy nor the people are the real problem. Organizational Refraction names what is.

Organizations are layered media. Strategic intent travels through structure, hierarchy, culture, process, and incentives — and at each boundary it bends, the way light bends moving between materials of different density. The distortion isn't a moral failure or a talent gap. It's a structural property of the organization, and it's measurable.

The book introduces the first structural causal model of strategic signal degradation: where intent bends, by how much, and what actually corrects the path.

## 2. What you'll learn — chapter overview

**Section header:** Four parts: diagnose the gap, understand the mechanism, then correct it.

### Part One — The Mechanism
- **Ch 1 · The Strategy-Reality Gap: A New Explanation.** Why organizations realize, on average, only about two-thirds of the value their strategies promise — and why "execute harder" has never closed that gap.
- **Ch 2 · What Existing Frameworks Get Right — and What They Miss.** Five decades of management theory, and the one question — a physics of transmission — that none of it answers.
- **Ch 3 · The Physics of Organizational Refraction.** The working vocabulary: structural density, refractive index, angle of incidence, and the boundaries where intent bends.

### Part Two — Three Modes of Refraction
- **Ch 4 · Hierarchical Refraction.** How intent bends going down the layers — and how ground truth bends coming back up.
- **Ch 5 · Cultural Refraction.** When the culture sincerely believes it's doing exactly what you asked, and isn't.
- **Ch 6 · Process Refraction.** When the metric quietly becomes the strategy and no one notices for a decade.

### Part Three — Compound Refraction
- **Ch 7 · When Layers Combine.** How distortions stack — two failures in one organization, years apart, from the same structural cause.
- **Ch 8 · Total Internal Reflection.** The strategy everyone endorses and no one implements — intent that never penetrates the boundary at all.

### Part Four — Diagnosing and Reducing Refraction
- **Ch 9 · Mapping Your Organization's Refractive Profile.** The refraction audit — moving the diagnostic target from symptoms to medium.
- **Ch 10 · Correcting the Angle.** Three structural levers that work — and why content-level fixes keep failing.
- **Ch 11 · Designing Refraction-Resistant Organizations.** Structure as a transmission medium, not a control architecture.
- **Ch 12 · Leadership as Optics Work.** Ensuring intent arrives intact at the point of action.

## 3. Who it's for

**Section header:** Built for leaders who own the gap.

**This book is for you if you are:**
- A CEO, COO, Chief Strategy Officer, or EVP/SVP of strategy or operations who is personally accountable for turning decisions into outcomes.
- Leading an organization of roughly 500+ employees where strategy has to cascade through multiple layers before it reaches the front line.
- In a sector where that cascade is real and costly — financial services, industrials, healthcare systems, professional services, large consumer brands.
- Already past the obvious fixes: you've run the off-sites, deployed the scorecards, hired the consultants, and the gap persists.

**It's probably not for you if:**
- You run an early-stage startup or a lean, flat organization where what you decide reaches the front line without passing through intermediary layers.
